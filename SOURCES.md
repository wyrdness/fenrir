# Sources & References

## Ancient Primary Sources

### Poetic Edda (13th century, compiled from earlier oral tradition)

- **Völuspá** (The Prophecy of the Seeress). Verses 40-44, 53. The primary source for Ragnarök prophecy including Fenrir's role in killing Odin.
- **Grímnismál** (The Lay of Grímnir). Verse 39. References to Fenrir's binding.
- **Lokasenna** (Loki's Quarrel). References to Loki's monstrous children.
- **Vafþrúðnismál** (The Lay of Vafþrúðnir). Verse 46-47. Odin's fate at Fenrir's jaws.

### Prose Edda by Snorri Sturluson (~1220 CE)

- **Gylfaginning** (The Beguiling of Gylfi). Chapters 13, 25, 34, 38, 51. Comprehensive account of:
  - Fenrir's birth and parentage
  - His raising in Asgard
  - The binding attempts (Læðingr, Dromi, Gleipnir)
  - Týr's sacrifice
  - Ragnarök prophecy and Fenrir's death
- **Skáldskaparmál** (The Language of Poetry). Additional references.

### Other Sagas

- **Heimskringla** by Snorri Sturluson. References to Fenrir in context of Norse cosmology.
- **Völsunga saga** (13th century). Brief references.
